What is Rotoscoping?

Rotoscoping is the art of tracing over live-action footage, frame by frame, to create stunningly fluid animations. Think of it as the bridge between reality and imagination — where every movement breathes with organic life. ✨

Creative Artist

Rotoscoper · 3D Designer · Space Nerd

Hey there! I'm a passionate animator and 3D artist obsessed with the intersection of hand-drawn animation and digital cosmos. I believe every frame tells a story — whether it's a dancing figure traced from life, or a planet spinning in virtual space.

My love for rotoscoping started with a single flip-book and grew into full animated shorts. My solar system work started as a school project and evolved into an interactive digital experience. Two worlds, one soul. ✨

🎞️Rotoscoping

Frame-by-frame tracing over video to produce fluid, lifelike animation.

🪐3D Modeling

Building planetary systems and environments in Blender & Cinema 4D.

🌈Color Theory

Crafting vibrant palettes that evoke emotion and guide the eye.

⚡After Effects

Motion graphics, compositing, and post-production magic.

🎵Sound Sync

Aligning animation rhythm to music for a multi-sensory experience.

🔭Space Science

Using real orbital mechanics & NASA data to power realistic simulations.

Fun Facts ✦

🎬 How I started rotoscoping

It all started with a childhood obsession with a Rotoscope scene in a classic film. I paused every frame and traced it on tracing paper. 500 sheets later, I had my first animation!

🌍 My favourite planet

Saturn — obviously. Those rings look hand-drawn by the universe itself. Nature's own rotoscoping masterpiece.

✨ Dream project

A full-length animated short set entirely in space, combining rotoscoped astronauts against 3D rendered nebulas. Coming soon... maybe. 🚀
